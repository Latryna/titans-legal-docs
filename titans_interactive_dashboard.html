From Perception to Agency

TITANS is a five-module cognitive architecture designed to bridge the gap between raw sensory input and autonomous, goal-directed action. This interactive dossier provides an overview of its core components, key scientific innovations, and strategic roadmap, intended for grant review boards and scientific collaborators.

The Cognitive Loop

This is the heart of TITANS: a continuous cycle of processing that mirrors a cognitive thought process. Click on any module to explore its function and the technology behind it.

M5: Agency

Bayesian Actor-Critic model for decision-making under uncertainty.

M1: Perception

Capsule Networks (CapsNet) to process raw data, preserving spatial hierarchies.

Key Innovations & Demonstrations

These core concepts distinguish TITANS from conventional AI systems and form the basis of our proposed research and white papers. Each represents a testable hypothesis with a clear demonstration path.

Epistemic Reward

The agent is intrinsically motivated to reduce its own uncertainty. The reward is proportional to the variance in its critic networks' predictions, driving exploration of unfamiliar states.

Cognitive Trace

A proposed visualization tool (`cognitive_trace.ipynb`) to demonstrate the data flow through the M1-M5 loop in a single timestep, making the system's "thought process" transparent.

Analogical Leap

A test for the reasoning module. The system must solve analogical puzzles by identifying relationships in its knowledge graph, demonstrating abstract reasoning beyond pattern matching.

Project Roadmap & TRL

The project is structured in four engineering sprints designed to advance the system from a conceptual framework (TRL 3) to a validated prototype in a relevant environment (TRL 6).

Epistemic Status & Risk Assessment

We maintain a clear distinction between established facts, testable hypotheses, and narrative goals. This framework informs our risk matrix, aligning with the EU AI Act's requirements for transparency and robustness.
